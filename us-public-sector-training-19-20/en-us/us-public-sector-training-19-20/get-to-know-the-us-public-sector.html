Overview of the US Public Sector Platform

The US public sector platform, which consists of Salesforce Government Cloud and MuleSoft Government Cloud, allows federal, state, and local governments to use Salesforce trusted and secure services. As an employee with access to Salesforce Government Cloud services, you have privileged access to critical infrastructure, networks, and data associated with government customers. Let’s dig a bit deeper into what makes the US public sector platform unique.

US public sector customers have unique regulatory and security compliance requirements. Data stored on the US public sector platform must be adequately maintained and secured to meet these special compliance requirements. These compliance standards include requirements from Federal Risk and Authorization Management Program (FedRAMP), along with the Department of Defense (DoD) Cloud Computing Security Requirements Guide, and Internal Revenue Service (IRS) Publication 1075. For more information on what these and other compliance frameworks are and how Salesforce meets them, visit the Salesforce Compliance site. 

We mentioned that those who work on Salesforce Government Cloud services have privileged access to critical security systems, infrastructure, networks, and accounts containing government data. This access makes us a primary target for cybercriminals and foreign intelligence agencies. Cybercriminals can target us to gain access to our credentials, laptops, and mobile devices and to gain insight into the US public sector platform, its customers, and the data stored there. 

That’s why it’s critical to be aware of, understand, and follow the heightened security, privacy, and compliance measures our US government customers require, as well as what threats to Salesforce Governments Services may look like.
